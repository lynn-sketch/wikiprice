Safe Shopping Guidelines

Protect yourself from scams when shopping in Kampala markets and on TikTok

How to Spot a Scam

  • Prices 50%+ below market average — An iPhone at UGX 150,000 when real price is UGX 450,000+ is a red flag.
  • Phone number only, no shop location — Legitimate Kikuubo sellers have arcade name, building, floor, and stall number.
  • Full payment upfront via mobile money — Scammers pressure you to pay before seeing the product.
  • No physical address — "Meet at taxi park" or "nationwide delivery only" with no fixed shop.
  • New accounts with no shop videos — Accounts created days ago with low followers and no video history showing a physical shop.
  • Pressure tactics — "Limited stock!" or "Offer ends today!" to rush your decision.

How to Shop Safely

  • Visit the physical shop before paying large amounts. WikiPrice provides specific locations — use them.
  • Pay on delivery, not upfront. Especially for online-only sellers.
  • Ask for a video call to see the product before paying.
  • Check TikTok history — Do their videos show their actual shop?
  • Start with a small purchase to test reliability before larger transactions.
  • Look for the green check on WikiPrice — verified means we sent someone to the shop.
  • Check wholesale vs retail — UGX 30,000 might be wholesale price for 12 pieces, not single piece.

What to Do If You Are Scammed

  1. Report on WikiPrice — Use the Report button on the deal or seller page.
  2. Report to police — File a report with Uganda Police.
  3. Report to TikTok — Report the account on TikTok.
  4. Warn other buyers — Leave a review warning others about the seller.
